Sarojini Naidu

Early Life

Sarojini Naidu 13 February 1879 ,2 March 1949 was an Indian political activist and poet who served as the first Governor of United Provinces, after India's independence. She played an important role in the Indian independence movement against the British Raj. She was the first Indian woman to be president of the Indian National Congress and appointed as governor of a state.

political Career

Beginning in 1904, Sarojini Naidu emerged as a prominent orator, passionately advocating for Indian independence and women's rights, particularly emphasizing the importance of women's education. Her speeches often followed the five-part rhetorical structures of Nyaya reasoning. In 1906, she delivered influential addresses at both the Indian National Congress and the Indian Social Conference in Calcutta. Recognized for her social activism, Naidu was awarded the prestigious Kaisar-i-Hind Medal in 1911 for her efforts in flood relief. However, she later returned this honor in protest against the April 1919 Jallianwala Bagh massacre, demonstrating her unwavering commitment to justice and human rights.
